Underage Drinking in the City of Toronto

A simple analysis of high schools within Toronto and their relative
proximity to drinking places and liquor stores

Underage drinking poses various risks to youth in Toronto. The consumption of alcohol by individuals below the legal drinking age of 19 is illegal in Ontario. Engaging in underage drinking can have detrimental effects on physical and mental health, as well as overall well-being. It can increase the likelihood of accidents, impaired judgment, risky behavior, academic problems, and potential addiction or dependency issues. The City of Toronto and organizations work to educate youth about the risks associated with underage drinking and provide resources for prevention and support.This map intends to show the risk of underage access to alcohol by illustrating all the different liquor stores, drinking places (bars, pubs, clubs etc.) and high-risk high schools within the city to visually assess the accessibility of alcohol from high schools. It also factors in the underage population density in Toronto as of 2016.
