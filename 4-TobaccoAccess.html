Accessibility to Tobacco Shops in Toronto

An analysis of access to tobacco for students within the City of Toronto

Access to tobacco for high school students in Toronto is tightly regulated to discourage youth smoking and prioritize public health. The legal age to purchase and possess tobacco products is 19 in Ontario, applying to all individuals, including high school students. The city enforces measures such as prohibiting the sale of tobacco to minors and requiring age verification through photo identification. Additionally, smoking and vaping are generally not allowed within a certain distance from school properties to create a smoke-free environment and reduce secondhand smoke exposure for students. This map intends to show the risk of underage access to tobacco by illustrating the easy accessibility to from high schools and the density of tobacco outlets by neighbourhood. It also highlights the low-income neighbhourhoods with easy access.
